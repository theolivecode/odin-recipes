French Toast

Description

French toast is a classic and beloved breakfast dish known for its sweet, rich flavor and satisfying texture. It's typically made by soaking slices of bread in a mixture of beaten eggs and milk, then frying them in a pan until golden brown. The outside becomes slightly crispy, while the inside remains soft and moist. Often served with toppings like syrup, powdered sugar, fruit, or butter, French toast offers a versatile and delicious start to the day. This dish can be made with various types of bread and customized with spices like cinnamon or vanilla for added flavor.

Ingredients

  • 2/3 cup milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (Optional)
  • salt to taste
  • 6 thick slices bread
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter, or more as needed

Steps

  1. Start by assembling all your required ingredients.
  2. In a shallow bowl, combine milk, eggs, a dash of vanilla, a sprinkle of cinnamon, and a pinch of salt, whisking them together thoroughly.
  3. Preheat a skillet or griddle over a medium-high flame, lightly coated with butter.
  4. Dip each slice of bread fully in the egg mixture, ensuring both sides are well soaked.
  5. Place the soaked bread in the preheated skillet, frying it until it turns a rich golden color, which should take about 3 to 4 minutes on each side.
  6. Once cooked, serve the French toast immediately while it's still warm.
